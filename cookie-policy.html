Cookie Policy

Last updated: April 14, 2026

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you visit a website. They allow websites to remember information about your visit, such as your preferences and browsing history. Cookies are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and to provide information to the owners of the site.

2. How We Use Cookies

Unisonix LLC uses cookies for various purposes to enhance your experience on our website:

  • Essential Cookies: These are necessary for the website to function properly. They enable basic functions like page navigation and access to secure areas.
  • Performance Cookies: These cookies collect information about how visitors use our website, such as which pages are visited most often and if visitors receive error messages.
  • Functional Cookies: These cookies remember your preferences and choices to provide a more personalized experience.
  • Marketing Cookies: These cookies track your activity across websites to deliver targeted advertisements based on your interests.
  • Analytics Cookies: These cookies help us understand visitor behavior and website performance through tools like Google Analytics.

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 Session Cookies

Session cookies are temporary and are deleted when you close your browser. They are used to maintain your session on our website and remember your actions during your visit.

3.2 Persistent Cookies

Persistent cookies remain on your device for a specified period or until you manually delete them. They help us recognize you on subsequent visits and remember your preferences.

3.3 First-Party Cookies

These are cookies set by Unisonix LLC directly on your device when you visit our website.

3.4 Third-Party Cookies

These are cookies set by third-party services integrated into our website, such as analytics providers, advertising networks, and social media platforms.

4. Specific Cookies We Use

  • _ga, _gat, _gid: Google Analytics cookies for tracking website usage
  • PHPSESSID: Session identifier for maintaining user sessions
  • unisonix_preferences: Stores your website preferences and settings
  • unisonix_tracking: Tracks user interactions for analytics purposes
  • fbp, _fbp: Facebook cookies for advertising and analytics

5. Cookie Consent

When you first visit our website, we display a cookie consent banner. By continuing to use our website, you consent to our use of cookies as described in this policy. You can withdraw your consent at any time by adjusting your cookie settings.

6. Managing Your Cookies

You have the right to control and manage cookies on your device. Most browsers allow you to refuse cookies or alert you when cookies are being sent. Here's how to manage cookies in popular browsers:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Preferences → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Cookies and website data
  • Edge: Settings → Privacy, search, and services → Clear browsing data

7. Do Not Track

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" feature. Our website currently does not respond to Do Not Track signals. However, you can control cookie usage through your browser settings as described above.

8. Third-Party Cookie Policies

Third-party services used on our website may have their own cookie policies. We encourage you to review their policies:

  • Google Analytics Cookie Policy
  • Facebook Cookie Policy
  • Twitter Cookie Policy

9. Cookie Retention

We retain cookies for different periods depending on their purpose:

  • Session cookies: Deleted when you close your browser
  • Preference cookies: Retained for up to 2 years
  • Analytics cookies: Retained for up to 26 months
  • Marketing cookies: Retained for up to 13 months

10. Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Know what cookies are being used on our website
  • Accept or reject cookies (except essential cookies)
  • Delete cookies from your device
  • Opt-out of marketing and analytics cookies
  • Request information about how your data is used

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons. We will notify you of any material changes by updating the "Last updated" date at the top of this policy.
